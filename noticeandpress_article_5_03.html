[Press Release] Kim Hashi Dae-dong-myeon Gangrene Illegal Cat Factory Detection

Registration Date 2020-06-30 04:53:54 PM Author Life Attachment Notice_and_press5_03.jpg

On May 28, 2020, the animal protection organization Life (hereinafter referred to as Life), together with the animal welfare team of Kimhae City Hall, detected an illegal animal production facility (cat) in Daedong-myeon-myeon-myeong-myeong-myeong-myeong-

The facility in which the suspect was caught allegedly made illegal profits by renovating 2 vinyl houses about 7 years ago to create animal breeding facilities, breeding more than 110 breeds of cats without obtaining an animal production license, and selling their cubs to pet shops and auction houses. The exact amount of illegal proceeds will be revealed by a police investigation.

Many of the more than 110 cats found at the scene were showing symptoms of skin disease, herpetic virus, blood dripping from their noses, and cats with severe damage to their eyeballs. In accordance with the Animal Protection Act, 29 cats were placed in emergency quarantine in the first place, of which 10 cats with severe conditions were transported to 3 partner hospitals in Life for treatment.

In addition, a large number of vaccines, antibiotics and syringes used were found in the field, which can be seen as key evidence of self-care, a violation of the Veterinarian Act that prohibits self-care. Indiscriminate self-care will result in serious harm to the animal's health, which will have to be followed by a thorough inspection by the relevant authorities.

In a world where meat, fish, and even eggs that are not in a living state can be raised in a certain environment through a production history system, where they were slaughtered, and who packed them, the offspring of poisonous living dogs and cats have no idea who raised them and how. Authorities must make a diagnosis as to whether this current system is encouraging the illegal animal production industry.

In order to prevent such a problem, Life's representative Sim In-sup said that in the end, it is necessary to amend the current Animal Protection Act to strengthen the licensing and punishment standards of the animal production industry, and to ensure the transparent management of the animal production industry through the animal production history system. We urge relevant discussions in the 21st National Assembly.

The accused caught today will be investigated by the police for violating Article 8 of the Animal Protection Act (prohibition of cruelty to animals, etc.), Article 33 of the Animal Protection Act (Registration of Business) and Article 10 of the Veterinary Act (prohibition of unlicensed practice).
